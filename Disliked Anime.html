Hitman Reborn

A boy, Tsunayoshi "Tsuna" Sawada, is chosen to become the tenth boss of the Vongola Family, as he is the great-great-great grandson of the first Vongola boss—who moved to Japan from Italy. Timoteo, the Vongola IX—the current head of the family—, sends Reborn, an infant hitman from Italy, to train the reluctant Tsuna.

What's so Bad about it

The First Arc is Mainly Consistent of Comedy and Slice of Life Chapters where it is the biggest weaknest of the series for me personally because I do not think the author is particularly good at writing comedy and most of the humor revolves around Tsuna getting tortured for the sake of the joke and it does not make it anymore entertaining. The Comedy is the weakest part for me since I do not Amano style of humor to be funny in anyway.

Other Critics

The Whole Cast is very Flat to me. The characters are purely defined by their gags and it gets very repetitive really quick for me and beyond that they do not have very satisfying character centric arcs and they end up being forgettable. Female characters also end up being very forgettable and do not serve the plot in any significant way.

The Serious Arcs after the comedy do not really land in a very satisfying way for me personally. After the Xanxus Arc, The Arcs that follow it have really cool set ups that keep me engaged but they fail to pay off the build up in any signficant way. Also the antagonists of the arcs are very forgettable and the groups do not feel like characters but plot devices.

Art Critics

The Anime Adaptation is very lackluster especially with the action. One good thing is cutting down the beginning section of the manga. The Animation is nothing to write home about and does not elevate the source material for me.

One Good thing I can say is that the Artwork for the series has a lot style. The Designs are very fashionable and aesthically pleasing to look at and have a lot of impressive illustrations. And the Fignts look verk bombastic in this particular artstyle.

Overall Story is has a lot of cool ideas but unstaisfying payoffs, The Characters are very flat and are very underdeveloped, and the Overall plot structure leaves a lot to be desired.

Fairy Tail

The world of Earth-land is home to numerous guilds where wizards[b] apply their magic for paid job requests. Natsu Dragneel, a Dragon Slayer wizard from the Fairy Tail guild, explores the Kingdom of Fiore in search of his missing adoptive father, the dragon Igneel. During his journey, he befriends a young celestial wizard named Lucy Heartfilia and invites her to join Fairy Tail. Natsu, Lucy, and the cat-like Exceed Happy form a team, which is joined by other guild members: Gray Fullbuster, an ice wizard; Erza Scarlet, a magical knight; and Wendy Marvell and Carla, another Dragon Slayer and Exceed duo. The team embarks on numerous missions, which include subduing criminals, illegal dark guilds, and ancient Etherious demons created by the dark wizard Zeref.

What's so bad about it

There a lot of a lot of things that I found very disappointing in this series that go from the writing, characters, how magic is used and implented, etc. Firstly, The Overall writing quality of the series can be very inconsistent like there can be plenty of good aspects but that get overshadowed but a lot of annoying aspects. I feel some of the story arcs can value emotions of the scenes instead of the logiciscal consistency. The Overall series can suffer from having a lot of plot holes and contrivances and thinking about the plot can expose some of the flaws in Mashima style of storytelling that prevalent in his current series Eden Zero.

Other Critics

The music in the sound tracks go on to be about are able to fit well with the action scenes and are considered to be staples of the series. Also, The Magic System starts off simple like Fire magic, Ice magic, Earth Magic, etc. Then it introduces God slayer Magic and other magic systems that are told to be stronger types of magic but easily get overpowered by the chrarcaters. I personally feel that it's not used to its creative potential as it could be since the fights end in a very basic way.

The Characters can be either the strongest aspect of the series or the weakest aspect depending on what you value in the story. The Characters are balanced to being either very serious or humorous depending on the tone and they can work. But they can be seen as simplistic and just very gag center and not engaging.

Art Critics

The Animation for the Anime can have it's ups and downs in quality. The Animation for the first series by A1 Pictures that ran from 2009-2013 was consistent and colorful looking and had more sakuga for the magic. The Animantion for the 2nd Series that ran from 2014-2016 had more darker color palattes which I personally liked and consider it the best season. The Final Season has the worst quality of Animation where it is very much animated as a powerpoint. A lot of the action scenes are still shots and impact frames with speed frames.

The Stakes for some of the Arcs can be inconsistent, especially the final war arc, in how it affects the overall world of the series which leads into another issue. A lot of the Arcs are self contained adventures and/or events of the series that focus a lot of character building in the first series but does not affect overall world until the 2nd series and final season. The Worldbuiding is the weakest aspect of the series for me since it feels very underdeveloped in terms of having any notable locations/landmarks.

Overall Story does not start to be overarching until way later on and does not establish enough overarching plot to invest in long time engagement since the beginning of the series is character centric. Story can suffer a lot from plot holes, contrivances, anti climatic events. The Powerscaling can be VERY inconsistent and not fit the show internalized logic.

Platinum End

Mirai Kakehashi is a young orphaned high school student who lives with his abusive aunt and uncle after the death of his parents. One day, Mirai decides he cannot take it anymore and attempts to commit suicide, but is saved by a Guardian Angel called Nasse, who also gives Mirai special powers.To make matters worse, not only is Mirai forced to take part in the contest to decide the next God, but some of the other candidates will do anything to win, including killing all of the other candidates as soon as possible.

What's so bad about it

This Anime was an Immense disappointment due to the fact it has the creators of Death Note and Bakuman which are highly regarded works for their chrarcaters and overall plot. This work completely disregards the talent that went into those works and opts to based off the narrative off the death game style of story with only a few intresting ideas. The Synopsis is very basic like other death game works like Mirai Nikki that it almost feels like a ripoff if you are not familar.

Other Critics

The music in the OP is the only thing I can consider very memorable due to the fact it is by Band Maid who have done other anime music for other shows.

The characters are very basic and bland. The MC is title tragic hero and has nothing going on writing wise and does not push the story. The Female character only exists to be romantic with the MC. The antagonists are very basic in their motives and personality in how they want to become god. The Scientist was the only intresting character in terms of antagonists with his philosopy on if god is needed and the Guy who was fighting cancer with a family to protect since he was to endure with.

Art Critics

The Anime Adaptation does not elevate the manga and is not worth watching due to the Animantion being very low quality and the visuals not matching the quality of the manga.

Overall Story is not engaging from a plot and character standpoint since it does not offer anything intresting to the formula and it makes it extra disappointing knowing who the creators are and their extensive background.
